Aerodynamics: Horizontal Wind Tunnel Challenges

Elementary, Middle, High

Science, Math, Engineering

1 hour

E4E

Description

Would your students be excited about interacting with a roaring blast of wind? In this workshop, students will learn about aerodynamics while building clay "cars" that woosh on a track when the air is turned on. Less aerodynamic cars will zoom to the end of the track, while more aerodynamic cars will move much less. Students will be challenged to have their car withstand the wind by only moving into certain ranges. While having fun with experimentation and building, students will also graph their results and interpret the graphs to improve their next iterations.

Materials + Preparation

This workshop requires a wind tunnel orientated in a horizontal position and a more complicated set-up than the companion wind tunnel workshop oriented in a vertical position. The Teacher's Guide to the vertically oriented workshop is more easily implemented and includes information about making a wind tunnel.

This poster gives more information on this workshop and the pedagogies used to develop it. A more detailed Teacher's Guide is not yet available.
