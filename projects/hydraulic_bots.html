Description

In this workshop, students collaborate to build hydraulic machines from popsicle sticks, dowels, wooden blocks, liquid-filled syringes, and hot glue guns. Students can elect to make a hydraulic battle bot to compete to knock down its rivals, a hydraulic machine to hit a ball, or any other creation. Students will enjoy designing their own creations and challenging their friends to duels while learning about hydraulics as well as friction and surface area.

There are many online resources describing how to build hydraulic arms or hydraulic bots, how to organize battles between them, and what materials are provided.

At different points in time, teams of Olin students from both Mathematics and Engineering for Everyone and Shifting Rhythms worked on refining materials for these activities and practicing using student-centered pedagogies when running these workshops.
