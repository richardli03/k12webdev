The Math Behind Our Democracy: Representation

Middle, High

Math

1 hour

E4E

Description

Who gets a say in democracy? Why does every vote in Wyoming count double when compared to certain other states? Representation in the US is skewed based on where you live and who draws your district boundaries. States with small populations have an outsized place in the senate and electoral college. If every state was as big as one person’s vote, the US would look very different! In this workshop, you’ll use a freely available website (https://go-cart.io/cartogram) to make maps to visualize how changing the data impacts each state - and what that means for democracy.

This workshop pairs with Math Behind Our Democracy: Gerrymandering.

Materials + Preparation

The Teacher's Guide provides tips for running this workshop with your students. The Slides can be modified to work in an online or in-person setting.

Whether this workshop is taken in-person or online, students will need access to the internet on a laptop/Chromebook/desktop in order to create maps using a free website. A phone can work, but the visualizations are easier to see on a larger screen.
