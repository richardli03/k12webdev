Mathematics and Engineering for Everybody

After years of running one-off K12 outreach events, Professor Sarah Spence Adams decided to create a course, Mathematics and Engineering for Everyone, wherein Olin students design, develop, and deploy STEM workshops with younger partner learners in a nearby city.

The community impact goal of this course is to increase interest, confidence, and sense of belonging in STEM among kids, with an emphasis on kids who may not otherwise have access to STEM materials & mentors. The learning goals for Olin students include practicing the user-centered engineering design process and learning about and implementing research-backed pedagogies and educationaldesign principles, while also improving their communicate and collaboration skills.

Materials created by students in Mathematics and Engineering for Everyone are tagged with Engineering for Everyone (E4E) in the Gallery. All materials are free to use under the Creative Commons Attribution-NonCommercial_ShareAlike 4.0 International license.

For more information on this course, please see this article.

Professor Sarah Spence Adams has been teaching math and engineering at Olin since 2002. She has been doing work related to K12 education for many years, including involving Olin students in K12 outreach initiatives, volunteering as a classroom assistant in grades 1-9, creating professional development programs for high school teachers, and serving on the Board of Directors for a K-8 school. Developing and teaching Mathematics and Engineering for Everyone has been one of the most rewarding and memorable parts of her career.
