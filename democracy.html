The Math Behind Our Democracy: Representation

Middle, High

Math

1 hours

Who gets a say in democracy? Why does every vote in Wyoming count double when compared to certain other states? Representation in the US is skewed based on where you live and who draws your district boundaries. States with small populations have an outsized place in the senate and electoral college. If every state was as big as one person’s vote, the US would look very different! In this workshop, you’ll use a freely available website (https://go-cart.io/cartogram) to make maps like these to visualize how changing the data impacts each state - and what that means for democracy.

This workshop pairs with The Math Behind Our Democracy: Gerrymandering.
