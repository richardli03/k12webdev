The Math Behind Our Democracy: Gerrymandering

Middle, High

Math

1 hour

Description

Who gets a say in democracy? How do politicians get to pick their voters? When district maps are drawn after every census, they are often designed to benefit one party. Sometimes this allows a minority of voters to elect a majority of seats. In the process, gerrymandering creates voting districts that look more like an inkblot test than a real map.
